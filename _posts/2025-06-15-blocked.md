Everything else is just screwing over everyone else on the way there."

    blocks:
      - type: text
        id: "tiles"
        title: "Tile Types"
        paragraphs:
          - "The board is built as you play. On your turn you draw a tile and place it adjacent to your current tile, then immediately move onto it and draw the corresponding card."
          - "Action Tile - Draw an action card when landed on."
          - "Board Tile - Draw a board card when landed on."
          - "Trap Tile - Draw a trap card when landed on."
          - "Bridge Tile - Pawns can only move in the direction of the arrows."
          - "Teleporter Tile - When two teleporter tiles are in play, pawns can teleport between the two."
          - "Star Tile - The goal of the game, reach this tile to win."
          - "Spawn Tiles - The colored spawns for each pawn."

      - type: text
        id: "cards"
        title: "Cards"
        paragraphs:
          - "There are three card types. Action Cards let you do things like swap positions, undo turns, or play extra turns. Board Cards let you manipulate the playing area itself. Trap Cards are negative effect cards drawn when landing on a Trap Tile -- each trap card is color coded to a pawn color, and that pawn is immune to it. Colorless traps apply to everyone."
          - "Cards can be played at any point in the game, regardless of whose turn it is. If multiple cards are played at once they resolve in the order they were played. Players are only allowed to play Action Cards right before moving onto a newly placed tile."

      - type: text
        id: "action-cards-list"
        title: "60 Action Cards"
        paragraphs:
          - "Ghost - Walk through a wall and/or player this turn."
          - "Double Play - Play a second turn after your next turn."
          - "Switcheroo - Swap positions with another player on the board."
          - "Trap - Choose a player to draw a trap card."
          - "Oops! All Traps! - Everyone except you draws a trap card."
          - "Nuhuh! - Undo everything from the previous turn."
          - "No you! - Reflect an action card played on you back on the caster."
          - "No Thanks! - Cancel any card."
          - "Best Friends - Choose two players to be linked. They both draw the same type of cards."
          - "Heartbreak - Unlink any two players."
          - "No Take Backs - Swap hands with another player."
          - "Recall - Return to a tile you previously placed."
          - "Jackpot - Draw 3 cards of your choosing."
          - "Steal - Steal another player's hand."
          - "Draw - Draw 1 board card and 1 action card."

      - type: text
        id: "board-cards-list"
        title: "60 Board Cards"
        paragraphs:
          - "Swap - Swap two already placed tiles."
          - "Sinkhole - Remove a tile from the board."
          - "Move - Move an existing tile anywhere."
          - "Block - Place a wall on an existing tile."
          - "Fortify - Place two walls on any tile(s)."
          - "Renovations - Remove a wall from the board."
          - "Demolition Crew - Remove all walls from the board."
          - "Land Slide! - Shift the entire board by 1 in any direction. (Including walls and players, excluding base tiles.)"
          - "Earthquake - Remove all tiles and walls from the board. (Except base tiles & occupied tiles.)"

      - type: text
        id: "trap-cards-list"
        title: "40 Trap Cards"
        paragraphs:
          - "Scorched - Lose your next turn. (RED)"
          - "Burnt Out - You cant place a new tile next turn. (RED)"
          - "Ashes to Ashes - Reveal your hand. The other players vote on 1 card for you to discard. (RED)"
          - "Shocked - Lose your next turn. (YELLOW)"
          - "Short Circuit - Discard 2 cards. (YELLOW)"
          - "Surge - Everyone else draws 1 card of their choosing. (YELLOW)"
          - "Poisoned - Lose your next turn. (GREEN)"
          - "Venomous Deal - Let another player take one of your cards at random. (GREEN)"
          - "Slow Toxin - For your next turn you may only move OR draw. (GREEN)"
          - "Frozen Solid - Lose your next turn. (BLUE)"
          - "Slippery Floor - Move back towards the nearest spawn 3 tiles. (BLUE)"
          - "Ice Cage - Place 2 walls in the direction of the Star Tile on your current tile. (BLUE)"
          - "Respawn - Get sent back to your Spawn Tile. (COLORLESS)"
          - "Unlucky Draw - Everybody draw a trap card immediately. (COLORLESS)"
          - "Card Drain - Discard 2 cards. (COLORLESS)"
          - "Reversal! - All players move 1 tile back towards the nearest Spawn Tile. (COLORLESS)"

      - type: text
        id: "rules"
        title: "Rules"
        paragraphs:
          - "SOFT LOCKING: When placing walls and tiles, you can not soft lock another player. If a player has no where to move and cannot draw any cards in their turn, and also have no relevant cards to play, they may either: A. Remove a wall or tile in their path. B. Draw a card of their choosing. C. Teleport to their spawn."
          - "TRANSIT VS SETTLED: If a player's pawn is moved in a turn, they must draw the card of the tile they land on at the END of that move, regardless of how many times this happens in a single turn. If a pawn is moved several tiles in one action, it is considered to be in transit. Players do not draw cards whilst in transit, only when that specific move action is finished and they are considered 'settled'. Additionally, players cannot play cards whilst in transit."
          - "WHEN TO PLAY CARDS: Players can play cards at any point in the game, regardless of whose turn it is. If several cards are played at once, they go in a first in first out order, i.e. the first card played takes affect first, and all others follow."
          - "TILE REMOVAL: If the tile a pawn is on is moved or removed, the pawn moves to the nearest valid tile that is not the Star tile."
          - "TURN ORDER: On a players turn, they may either stay in place and draw the respective card of the tile they currently occupy, or they must draw a new tile and place it. Players must place new tiles adjacent to their current tile, and in a manner in which they can move on to it. Subsequently, the player must also immediately move onto that tile. Players are allowed to play strictly Action cards right before they move onto the new tile. Once on the new tile, they must draw the respective card of that tile."

  - title: "Final"

    id: "final-note"
    blocks:
      - type: text
        id: "final-note"
        paragraphs:
          - "This is still an early prototype with a lot of room to grow. On the gameplay side, the card pool needs a serious trim. Right now there are just too many cards, and narrowing it down to the best ones would make the game a lot tighter and less cumbersome to play through."
          - "On the production side, this was my first ever attempt at a physical game. The components are functional but clunky and pretty fragile. The tiles in particular are frustrating. Shuffling and storing them is a pain since they're rigid 3D printed pieces with insets and outsets to stack (though this doesn't work that well). One idea I'm considering is redesigning the tiles to be flat and the board itself to have holes for the tiles, then using a bag for the tile pool so you can just shake it to shuffle and reach in to draw at random. Adding more tile types to the pool could also make the game more interesting."
          - "There's a lot I want to do with this one. More playtesting, a cleaner card set, and a much better production pass are all on the list. I'd also love to see this turn into a real board game. Im aware it's a game for masochists and it can very easily tear some friend groups apart, but I think that's part of its fun. My friend group and I have a lot of fun playing this even in it's prototype stage, so I believe that's a good sign. As an avid board game collector, I'd live to be able to say I made my own commercial board game."
